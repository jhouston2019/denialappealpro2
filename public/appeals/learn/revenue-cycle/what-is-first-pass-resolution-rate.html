Revenue Cycle

What Is First-Pass Resolution Rate?

First-pass resolution rate answers the ultimate billing question: how often does a claim get paid in full the very first time it is submitted — with no corrections, resubmissions, or appeals required?

Definition

The percentage of claims paid on the first submission without requiring resubmission or appeal — the primary indicator of billing department efficiency.

What First-Pass Resolution Rate Means

First-pass resolution rate, sometimes called first-pass acceptance rate, measures the proportion of claims that are fully paid upon initial submission. Unlike clean claim rate, which only tracks whether a claim is accepted for processing, first-pass resolution rate tracks the complete outcome — payment without any follow-up action. A claim that is accepted but then denied does not count toward first-pass resolution. A claim that is accepted and paid, even with an adjustment, may count depending on how your organization defines full payment. This metric is the gold standard for evaluating billing department performance because it captures the entire front-to-back efficiency of your claims process.

Why First-Pass Resolution Rate Matters

Every claim that fails first-pass resolution consumes staff time and delays cash flow. Reworking a rejected claim costs an estimated $25 per claim in labor. Filing an appeal costs $50 to $100 per claim when you factor in staff time, documentation gathering, and follow-up. A billing department resolving 85 percent of claims on first pass versus 92 percent on 5,000 monthly claims means 350 additional claims requiring rework every month — a difference of $8,750 to $35,000 in monthly labor cost alone. First-pass resolution rate is the single metric that best predicts both days in AR and overall billing department cost efficiency.

How First-Pass Resolution Rate Affects Your Practice

Low first-pass resolution rates force billing teams into a reactive cycle of corrections and appeals rather than proactive revenue optimization. Staff burnout increases when the majority of each day is spent fixing claims instead of preventing errors. Provider satisfaction drops when reimbursement delays create uncertainty about practice finances. Payer relationships suffer when high resubmission volumes trigger audits or increased scrutiny. Conversely, practices that consistently achieve 90 percent or higher first-pass resolution operate with lower billing costs, faster collections, and staff who can focus on complex cases rather than routine rework.

First-pass resolution rate and denial rate are two sides of the same coin. Improving one almost always improves the other — because denials are the primary reason claims fail to resolve on first pass.

What to Do About Low First-Pass Resolution

Identify the gap between your clean claim rate and first-pass resolution rate — this delta represents claims that are accepted but then denied or underpaid. Analyze denial reasons for these claims and address root causes in coding, authorization, and documentation. Invest in claims scrubbing technology and pre-submission edits. Track first-pass resolution by payer and provider to pinpoint specific problem areas. Frequently Asked Questions

How is first-pass resolution rate calculated?

First-pass resolution rate equals claims paid in full on the first submission divided by total claims submitted, expressed as a percentage. Claims requiring resubmission, correction, or appeal are excluded from the numerator.

What is a good first-pass resolution rate?

High-performing billing departments achieve a first-pass resolution rate of 90 percent or above. Rates below 85 percent indicate that too many claims require rework, driving up labor costs and days in AR.

How is first-pass resolution rate different from clean claim rate?

Clean claim rate measures whether claims are accepted without rejection at submission. First-pass resolution rate measures whether claims are fully paid without any follow-up action — including denials that occur after the claim is accepted for processing.

What improves first-pass resolution rate?

Strong front-end verification, accurate coding, prior authorization compliance, and automated claims scrubbing all improve first-pass resolution. Denial prevention at submission is the most cost-effective lever because appeals cost 10 to 25 times more than getting the claim right initially.
