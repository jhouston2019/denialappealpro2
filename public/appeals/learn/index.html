Denial Appeal Glossary

Insurance Denial & Appeal Terms Explained

Plain-language definitions for the codes, processes, and billing terms you encounter on every denied claim.

This glossary exists because insurance denials come wrapped in jargon — CARC codes, adjustment groups, remittance advice — that slows down your response when speed matters. It is built for billing professionals, revenue cycle teams, practice managers, and patients who need to understand what a denial means and what to do next, without wading through payer policy manuals.

Denial Codes

The standardized codes on your EOB that tell you exactly why a line item was denied or adjusted.

  • CARC CodeStandardized number on an EOB explaining why a claim or line item was adjusted or denied.
  • RARC CodeSupplemental remark code providing additional detail alongside a CARC code.
  • CARC 97Service bundled into another procedure already paid — the most common bundling denial.
  • CARC 50Medical necessity denial — payer determined the service was not required.
  • CARC 29Timely filing denial — claim submitted after the payer's deadline.
  • CARC 197Prior authorization missing, incomplete, or not applicable to the billed service.
  • CARC 4Modifier inconsistent with the procedure billed — modifier error denial.
  • CARC 96Non-covered charge — service not a benefit under the patient's plan.
  • CARC 18Exact duplicate claim — payer already processed an identical submission.
  • CO DenialContractual obligation adjustment — provider cannot bill the patient for this amount.
  • PR DenialPatient responsibility — deductible, copay, or coinsurance owed after adjudication.
  • OA DenialOther adjustment — catch-all category outside CO and PR responsibility.

Appeal Process

The formal steps, deadlines, and review types used to overturn a denied or reduced claim payment.

  • Insurance AppealFormal written request to a payer to reverse a denied or reduced claim payment.
  • First-Level AppealInitial internal review request submitted directly to the payer after a denial.
  • Second-Level AppealFollow-up appeal when first-level review is denied — reviewed by a different payer team.
  • External AppealIndependent review outside the insurance company — required by law in most states.
  • Independent Medical Review (IMR)Independent physician review of a medical necessity denial with no payer relationship.
  • Appeal DeadlineTime limit to submit an appeal after a denial — typically 30 to 180 days by payer.
  • Medical NecessityPayer determination that a service is appropriate and required for the patient's condition.
  • Peer-to-Peer ReviewDirect conversation between treating physician and payer medical director before formal appeal.
  • Remittance Advice (EOB)Payer document explaining how each claim was processed, paid, denied, and coded.
  • Claim AdjustmentAny payer change to the billed amount — reductions, denials, or patient responsibility.
  • Timely Filing LimitDeadline to submit a claim after date of service — missing it triggers CARC 29.
  • Appeal Success RatePercentage of denied claims overturned after formal appeal — 40% to 75% by denial type.
