Denial Codes

What Is CARC 18?

CARC 18 fires when the payer already has an identical claim on file — your resubmission matched the original on every key field, so the duplicate was rejected with zero payment.

Definition

Exact duplicate claim or service — the payer has already received and processed an identical claim.

What CARC 18 Means

CARC code 18 indicates that the payer has identified the submitted claim or service as an exact duplicate of one already received and processed. Payers maintain duplicate detection systems that match claims on patient identifier, date of service, provider NPI, CPT codes, and billed amounts. When a second identical submission arrives without a corrected claim indicator, the payer rejects it with CARC 18. This is not a clinical or coding denial — it is an administrative rejection telling you the payer already has this claim in their system.

Why CARC 18 Matters

Duplicate claim denials create confusion because the rejected submission may not be the problem — the original claim may still be pending, denied for a different reason, or paid at a lower amount than expected. Billing teams that resubmit denied claims without changing the claim frequency code or adding a corrected claim indicator trigger CARC 18 repeatedly, wasting clearinghouse fees and cluttering the payer's system. Duplicate submissions also raise compliance flags — payers monitoring for duplicate billing patterns may initiate audits when a provider generates more than 2% duplicate claim volume in a quarter.

How CARC 18 Affects Your Claim

CARC 18 results in zero payment on the duplicate submission. The original claim's status is unchanged — it remains whatever it was before the duplicate arrived (paid, denied, pending, or pended). If your team mistook a CARC 18 rejection as the primary denial, you may be appealing the wrong claim entirely. The duplicate itself requires no appeal — you need to locate and work the original claim. In some cases, the original was paid correctly and the duplicate was an unnecessary resubmission triggered by a billing system auto-retry. In others, the original was denied and the duplicate should have been submitted as a corrected claim with frequency code 7.

Never appeal a CARC 18 duplicate directly. Find the original claim first — your recovery path depends on whether the original was paid, denied, or is still pending.

What to Do About CARC 18

Search the payer portal or your practice management system for the original claim using patient account number, date of service, and claim control number. Review the original claim's status and remittance. If the original was denied, appeal or correct that claim — not the duplicate. If you need to resubmit with corrections, use claim frequency code 7 and include the original payer claim number. Implement front-end duplicate checks in your billing system to prevent matching submissions. If the payer shows no record of the original despite your duplicate rejection, call payer provider services with both submission confirmation numbers to reconcile the discrepancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does CARC 18 mean on a remittance?

CARC 18 means the payer identified the claim or service as an exact duplicate of one already received and processed. No additional payment is made on the duplicate submission.

Is CARC 18 the same as a resubmission?

Not always. A corrected claim with frequency code 7 is a resubmission, not a duplicate. CARC 18 applies when the same claim is submitted twice without indicating it replaces or corrects a prior submission.

How do I fix a CARC 18 duplicate denial?

First, locate the original paid or pending claim. If the original was denied in error, appeal the original claim rather than resubmitting. If the duplicate was accidental, void it and work the original claim status.

Can duplicate submissions cause compliance issues?

Yes. Repeated duplicate submissions can trigger payer audits and compliance reviews. Billing systems should flag claims with matching patient, date of service, and CPT codes before resubmission.
