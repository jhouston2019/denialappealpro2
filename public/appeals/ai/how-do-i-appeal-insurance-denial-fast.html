Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I submit an insurance appeal?

You can submit a first-level appeal within 24 hours if you have your denial letter and supporting records ready. The writing step takes the longest — typically 30 to 60 minutes manually — but AI tools reduce that to under 60 seconds.

What is the fastest way to write an appeal letter?

Upload your denial letter or EOB to an AI appeal generator that reads the CARC code and builds a targeted rebuttal. Denial Appeal Pro produces a submission-ready letter in under 60 seconds, eliminating the manual research and drafting that slows most appeals.

How long do I have to appeal a denied claim?

Most commercial payers allow 180 days from the denial date for a first-level appeal. Medicare allows 120 days. Missing the deadline is one of the top reasons appeals fail, so check your EOB for the exact filing window immediately.

Can I speed up the payer's appeal review?

You cannot control payer review timelines, which average 30 days for commercial plans. You can speed up your side by submitting a complete appeal with all attachments on the first submission — incomplete appeals trigger additional requests that add weeks.
