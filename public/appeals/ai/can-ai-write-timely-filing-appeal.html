Frequently Asked Questions

What is a CARC 29 timely filing denial?

CARC 29 means the payer denied the claim because it was not received within the required filing timeframe. Most commercial payers require submission within 90 to 365 days of the date of service. Medicare typically allows 12 months. The denial states the claim arrived after the deadline, even if your office believes it was filed on time.

What proof do I need for a timely filing appeal?

Clearinghouse acceptance reports showing the original submission date and time are the strongest evidence. Electronic confirmation numbers, fax transmission logs, and certified mail receipts also support timely filing appeals. Denial Appeal Pro structures your letter around the proof types available and prompts you to attach the relevant records.

Can I appeal a timely filing denial if I truly filed late?

Appeals are strongest when you have proof of timely submission that the payer failed to process. If the claim was genuinely filed late with no extenuating circumstances, overturn rates drop significantly. Some payers grant exceptions for payer-caused delays, system outages, or retroactive eligibility changes. Denial Appeal Pro identifies which argument applies to your situation.

How long do I have to appeal a timely filing denial?

Appeal deadlines for CARC 29 denials vary by payer, typically 30 to 180 days from the denial date. Missing the appeal deadline makes the denial permanent regardless of your submission proof. Denial Appeal Pro reads your denial and surfaces the appeal deadline so you can file before it expires.
