Frequently Asked Questions

What is an NCCI bundling denial?

An NCCI bundling denial occurs when a payer applies National Correct Coding Initiative edits and denies a procedure code because it is considered included in another code billed on the same date of service. This typically appears as CARC 97 on your remittance advice. The denied code was bundled into a primary procedure and payment was zeroed out.

Which modifiers does AI reference in a bundling appeal?

Denial Appeal Pro references modifiers 59, XE, XS, XP, and XU depending on the clinical scenario. Modifier 59 indicates a distinct procedural service. The X-modifiers provide greater specificity about why procedures are distinct. The AI selects the appropriate modifier rationale based on the procedure codes and denial reason on your EOB.

What documentation supports an NCCI bundling appeal?

Operative notes proving the procedures were performed at separate anatomic sites or during separate encounters are the strongest evidence. The appeal letter should reference the specific NCCI edit pair, explain clinical distinctness, and cite the modifier used on the original claim or request retroactive modifier application.

How often are CARC 97 bundling appeals successful?

CARC 97 appeals with operative notes demonstrating clinical distinctness and correct modifier usage overturn at rates of 35 to 50% at first level. Appeals without documentation proving the procedures were separate rarely succeed. A structured letter citing the NCCI edit and modifier rationale significantly improves outcomes.
