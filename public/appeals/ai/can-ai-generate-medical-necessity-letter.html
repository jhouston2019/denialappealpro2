Frequently Asked Questions

Which CARC codes does AI handle for medical necessity appeals?

AI generates medical necessity appeal letters for CARC 50 and related codes where the payer states the service is not medically necessary. The letter references LCD and NCD coverage criteria and structures the rebuttal around documented clinical findings required to meet each coverage requirement.

Does AI need my clinical notes to write a medical necessity letter?

The AI generates the appeal letter structure and references the documentation types your payer requires — progress notes, operative reports, lab results, imaging reports. You attach the actual clinical records when submitting. The letter tells the payer exactly where in those records the medical necessity criteria are met.

How is an AI medical necessity letter different from a template?

Generic templates use placeholder language that does not reference your specific procedure, diagnosis codes, or payer policy. Denial Appeal Pro reads your denial and generates a letter mapped to your CARC code, procedure codes, and the payer's stated denial reason — not a one-size-fits-all paragraph about medical necessity.

What is the overturn rate for medical necessity appeals?

First-level medical necessity appeals succeed at varying rates depending on documentation quality and payer, but well-documented CARC 50 appeals with operative notes and LCD-aligned clinical evidence overturn at rates of 40 to 55%. A structured letter that cites specific coverage criteria improves your odds compared to a generic request to reconsider.
