Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between CARC and RARC codes?

CARC codes are Claim Adjustment Reason Codes that explain why a claim or line item was denied or adjusted at the group level. RARC codes are Remittance Advice Remark Codes that provide additional detail about the adjustment. Denial Appeal Pro reads both code types and explains how they relate to the denial reason on your EOB.

Can AI explain multiple CARC codes on one denial?

Yes. Many denials carry two or more CARC codes — for example, CARC 50 for medical necessity combined with CARC 96 for non-covered charges. Denial Appeal Pro decodes each code, explains how they interact, and generates an appeal letter that addresses the primary denial driver.

Does AI explain what documentation I need for each CARC code?

Yes. Each CARC code maps to specific documentation requirements. CARC 97 needs NCCI modifier rationale and operative notes. CARC 29 needs clearinghouse submission timestamps. CARC 197 needs authorization records. Denial Appeal Pro tells you exactly which records to attach for the codes on your denial.

How many CARC codes exist?

The X12 standard defines over 300 CARC codes, though most denials cluster around 15 to 20 common codes like 50, 97, 29, 197, and 4. Denial Appeal Pro recognizes the full code set and provides plain-language explanations for any code appearing on your denial letter.
