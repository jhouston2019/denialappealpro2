Frequently Asked Questions

What does CARC 97 mean on my denial?

CARC 97 means the payer bundled your secondary procedure into the payment for another code on the same date of service. The bundled line item receives zero payment even though both codes were billed.

Which modifier do I need for a CARC 97 appeal?

Modifier 59 or XE, XP, XS, or XU indicates a distinct procedural service. Your appeal must explain why the procedures were clinically separate — different anatomic sites, sessions, or encounters — not just that you appended a modifier.

What documentation wins a CARC 97 appeal?

Operative reports or procedure notes showing each service was performed at a different anatomic site or during a distinct clinical session have the highest overturn rate. Include the NCCI edit pair reference and explain why the bundling edit should not apply.
