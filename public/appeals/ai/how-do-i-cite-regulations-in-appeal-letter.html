The Direct Answer

Cite regulations in an insurance appeal letter by referencing the specific LCD or NCD policy for Medicare claims, the payer's published medical policy for commercial plans, NCCI edit manual sections for bundling denials, and ACA external review rights for patient appeals. Appeals that quote payer policy language alongside clinical evidence overturn at rates 15 to 20 percentage points higher than appeals using generic medical necessity arguments alone.

How It Works

Identify the regulatory framework that applies to your denial type. For Medicare medical necessity denials, search the CMS Coverage Database for the LCD or NCD governing the denied CPT code. For commercial payer denials, locate the payer's medical policy document on their provider portal. For CARC 97 bundling denials, cite the specific NCCI edit pair from the CMS NCCI Policy Manual.

Quote the exact policy language in your appeal letter — policy number, effective date, and the specific coverage criteria or edit rule. Then map your clinical or billing evidence to each cited requirement. For patient appeals, cite 45 CFR 147.136 for external review rights if the internal appeal is denied. Frequently Asked Questions

Which regulations should I cite in a medical appeal letter?

Cite the applicable LCD or NCD for Medicare claims, the payer's medical policy for commercial plans, NCCI edit policies for bundling denials, and state prompt-pay statutes if the payer missed required response timelines.

How do I find the correct LCD or NCD for my appeal?

Search the CMS Medicare Coverage Database at cms.gov/medicare-coverage-database using the denied CPT or HCPCS code. Quote the policy number, effective date, and specific coverage criteria language in your appeal.

Can I cite the Affordable Care Act in my appeal?

Yes, for patient appeals. The ACA grants patients the right to an internal appeal and external independent review. Cite 45 CFR 147.136 for external review rights when your internal appeal is denied.
