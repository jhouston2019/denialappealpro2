First-Level Appeal Overturn Rates

Data from the American Hospital Association and HFMA consistently shows that 63% of provider-submitted first-level appeals are overturned fully or partially. That means nearly two out of three denied claims recover some payment when a written appeal is filed with supporting documentation. Patient-initiated appeals succeed at a lower rate — approximately 40% — largely because patients often lack the clinical records and billing terminology that strengthen provider-filed appeals.

Success Rates by Denial Type

Not all denials are equally appealable. Authorization denials where a valid auth number exists but was not transmitted succeed over 70% of the time. Timely filing denials with clearinghouse proof of submission overturn at similar rates. Medical necessity denials succeed 40 to 55% depending on documentation quality. Bundling denials with strong operative notes succeed roughly 50%. Knowing your denial type tells you whether the expected recovery justifies the appeal effort.

Practices that appeal consistently recover 15 to 20% of all initially denied dollars. Practices that write off denials without appealing recover 0%. The difference is process, not luck.

Second-Level and External Review

When first-level appeals fail, second-level internal appeals and independent external review add incremental recovery. External review for medical necessity denials under ACA-compliant plans succeeds approximately 50% of the time. Cumulative recovery across all appeal levels pushes total denial recovery to 15 to 20% of initially denied claim dollars for practices with structured appeal programs.
