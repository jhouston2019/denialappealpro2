Header and Claim Identification

Start with your practice letterhead, date, and payer appeals department address. In the first paragraph, identify the patient by name and member ID, the claim number, date of service, billed amount, and the CARC code from the denial. State clearly that you are filing a first-level appeal and request reprocessing of the claim. This opening paragraph gives the appeals reviewer everything they need to pull your claim file.

Denial Summary and Rebuttal

Restate the payer's denial reason in one sentence, then immediately counter it with your argument. Reference specific clinical findings, authorization numbers, or submission timestamps depending on the denial type. Use numbered points if you are addressing multiple issues on the same claim. Each point should connect a fact from your documentation to a reason the denial should be reversed.

The rebuttal section is the only part of the template that changes between denials. Match your argument to the CARC code — never submit a generic appeal letter for a specific denial reason.

Attachment List and Closing

List every document attached to the appeal by name: remittance advice, progress notes, authorization letter, operative report, or proof of timely filing. End with a direct request: reprocess the claim and issue payment within the payer's stated timeframe. Include your contact name, phone number, and email for the appeals reviewer to reach you with questions.

Template Section Breakdown

  • Paragraph 1: Patient ID, claim number, date of service, appeal level
  • Paragraph 2: Denial reason restated with CARC code cited
  • Paragraph 3: Rebuttal with evidence mapped to denial reason
  • Paragraph 4: Attachment list and reprocessing request
  • Closing: Contact information and provider signature
