Medical Necessity Denials

CARC 50 Medical Necessity Denial Appeal

CARC 50 accounts for nearly 12% of all claim denials. When a payer rejects a $2,000 MRI or a $400 office visit as not medically necessary, your appeal must connect diagnosis, treatment, and coverage criteria in writing.

CARC Code 50

These are non-covered services because this is not deemed a medical necessity by the payer.

Understanding Why Your Claim Got CARC 50

Payers apply medical necessity edits automatically when submitted diagnosis codes do not align with the billed procedure, when frequency limits are exceeded, or when the service falls outside Local Coverage Determination criteria. The denial is a data mismatch between what you billed and what the payer's medical policy allows — not necessarily a clinical judgment on your physician's decision.

What Your Appeal Letter Must Prove

A CARC 50 appeal succeeds when you demonstrate three things: the patient had a condition requiring the service, the specific procedure was the appropriate treatment, and the case meets the payer's published coverage criteria. Quote the relevant LCD or medical policy section and map your patient's clinical findings to each coverage requirement listed in that policy.

Generic statements like the service was clinically appropriate lose CARC 50 appeals. Quote specific findings from the medical record and match them line-by-line to the payer's coverage criteria.

Clinical Documentation Checklist

  • Progress notes with diagnosis, symptoms, and treatment rationale
  • Lab results, imaging reports, or pathology supporting the order
  • Physician attestation letter if notes alone are insufficient
  • LCD/NCD policy excerpt with patient-specific applicability

Escalation Options After First-Level Denial

If your written appeal is denied, request a peer-to-peer review with the treating physician. For Medicare claims, file a redetermination then reconsideration through the Qualified Independent Contractor. Each level adds clinical depth that automated edits cannot evaluate — and each level recovers revenue that would otherwise write off as bad debt.
